Audio

Volume

Adjust volume in 10% steps with ⌘↑ / ⌘↓, or use the slider in the bottom status bar. In full screen, the same control appears in the bottom overlay.

Sound Chip

Bubilator88 emulates the YM2608 (OPNA) sound chip:

ChannelDescription
FM6-channel, 4-operator FM synthesis
SSG3-channel PSG (AY-3-8910 compatible)
ADPCMPCM sample playback
Rhythm6 built-in rhythm instrument sounds

Individual source toggles are available in the DEBUG menu after enabling Settings > General > Show DEBUG Menu.

Audio Buffer

In Settings > Audio, you can adjust the audio buffer size from 20 ms to 500 ms. Lower values reduce latency but may cause crackling on slower systems.

FDD Sound

Enable Settings > Audio > Enable FDD Sound to play synthesized floppy seek and access sounds. Drive 1 and Drive 2 are panned slightly differently.

Volume can be set to one of three levels.

Use Output Device to route FDD sounds to a specific audio device, independent of the main audio output. This lets you hear mechanical drive sounds through the built-in speakers even when headphones are connected for the main audio. Choose System Default to follow the system output.

Pseudo Stereo

Enable Settings > Audio > Enable Pseudo Stereo to add a chorus-like stereo widening effect to mostly mono FM / SSG output.

This control is unavailable while Immersive Audio is enabled. On the FM side, the effect is suppressed once hardware panning is used.

Immersive Audio

Enable Settings > Audio > Enable Immersive Audio to place FM, SSG, ADPCM, and Rhythm in 3D space. Compatible headphones can also use head tracking.

The position pad moves each source's left/right pair together. Use Reset Positions to restore the defaults.

Recording

Use Control > Start Audio Recording (⇧⌘R) to capture the emulator's audio output to a file. While recording, a red ● indicator appears in the status bar — click it (or press ⇧⌘R again) to stop. The output folder opens in Finder once recording stops.

File names combine the mounted disk title and a timestamp (e.g. Ys-2026-04-24-123456.wav).

Formats

Pick from Settings > Audio > Recording > Format.

FormatExtensionNotes
WAV.wavUncompressed 16-bit PCM. Best for DAW import.
Apple Lossless.cafLossless compression. Typically 50–70% of WAV size.
AAC.m4aLossy compression. Always written as stereo (AAC cannot encode 8 discrete channels).

Channel Modes

The Channels picker chooses the recording layout (AAC is locked to stereo):

  • Separated (8ch): FM, SSG, ADPCM, and Rhythm each get their own stereo pair in one 8-channel file (ch0/1 FM, ch2/3 SSG, ch4/5 ADPCM, ch6/7 Rhythm). Intended for DAW workflows (Logic, Audacity) where you want to mix or edit each source independently. QuickTime Player and most media players will only play the first two channels.
  • Stereo (2ch): Records the final mix. Plays in any media player. If Pseudo Stereo is enabled, its effect is captured in the file.

Separated mode internally forces immersive-style per-channel output for the duration of the session, so Pseudo Stereo is not applied to the recording (by design — DAW users want the raw per-source signal).

Save Location

With Ask save location every time enabled, a folder-picker appears when recording starts. Otherwise recordings are written into the folder chosen via Choose... (default: ~/Music).

Notes

  • Quitting normally (⌘Q or via the menu) during a recording flushes the file automatically. If the app is force-quit or crashes, AAC (.m4a) files may be left without their trailing index atom and cannot be played back.
